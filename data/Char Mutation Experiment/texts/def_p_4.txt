Preschool education has become increasingly popular in recent years, with more and more parents opting to enroll their children in preschool programs. Many parents believe that preschool can provide their children with a solid foundation for future academic success. But does attending preschool actually make a difference in a child's performance in school later on?

Numerous studies have been conducted to investigate the impact of preschool education on children's academic achievement. Research consistently shows that children who attend preschool tend to do better in school compared to those who do not. One study published in the Journal of Educational Psychology found that children who attended preschool had higher math and reading scores in early elementary school compared to their peers who did not attend preschool. This suggests that preschool education can have a positive impact on children's academic performance.

One of the main reasons why preschool education is believed to be beneficial is that it can help children develop essential skills that are important for school readiness.
