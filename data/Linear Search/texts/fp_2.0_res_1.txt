Universities are institutions of higher learning that strive to provide equal opportunities for all students to succeed academically. However, this can prove challenging for disabled students who may require certain accommodations in order to fully participate and excel in their education. As such, the question arises: should universities provide accommodations for disabled students?

There are several arguments in favor of providing accommodations for disabled students at universities. First and foremost, it is a matter of accessibility and inclusivity. By providing necessary accommodations, universities create an environment where all students have the opportunity to learn and succeed regardless of any disabilities they may have. This promotes diversity on campus and ensures that every student has an equal chance at obtaining a quality education.

Furthermore, providing accommodations can help level the playing field for disabled students who face unique challenges in accessing educational resources or participating fully in classroom activities. Accommodations such as extended time on exams or assistive technology can help mitigate these challenges and allow disabled students to demonstrate their knowledge and abilities without being hindered by their disabilities.

In addition, not providing accommodations could put universities at risk of violating laws protecting individuals with disabilities from discrimination. The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requires public institutions like universities to make reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities so long as those accommodation do not impose undue burden on the institution.
