As our understanding of disabilities has evolved, so too have our societal norms and expectations regarding the treatment of individuals with disabilities. One area in which this evolution is evident is in the realm of higher education, where universities have increasingly been called upon to provide accommodations to disabled students.

There are several reasons why universities should provide accommodations to disabled students. First and foremost, it is a matter of equity and access. All students, regardless of their abilities, should have the opportunity to fully participate in and benefit from the educational opportunities that universities offer. By providing accommodations to disabled students, universities can help level the playing field and ensure that these students have the same opportunities for success as their peers.

Furthermore, providing accommodations to disabled students is not only a matter of fairness, but also of legal obligation. Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and other federal and state laws, universities are required to provide reasonable accommodations to disabled students in order to ensure their full and equal participation in the educational programs and activities offered by the institution. Failure to do so can result in legal consequences, including the loss of federal funding and the imposition of fines and penalties.

Moreover, providing accommodations to disabled students can also have a positive impact on the overall campus climate and culture. By demonstrating a commitment to inclusivity and accessibility, universities can create a more welcoming and supportive environment for all students, and foster a sense of community and belonging that benefits the entire campus community.
