In recent years, terrorism has become a major global issue, with attacks occurring in various countries around the world. There are a number of events and experiences that many experts and analysts agree are contributing to the increase in terrorism. These factors include political instability, foreign intervention, social and economic inequality, and the rise of extremist ideologies.

One of the main factors that experts believe are contributing to the increase in terrorism is political instability. Countries that are politically unstable, with weak governments and a lack of rule of law, are often at a higher risk of terrorism. Terrorist organizations are able to exploit the political instability in a country, using the chaos and lack of governance to carry out attacks and recruit new members. The power vacuums and lack of security in politically unstable countries are the perfect breeding grounds for terrorism to flourish.

Foreign intervention is another factor that many experts believe is contributing to the increase in terrorism.
