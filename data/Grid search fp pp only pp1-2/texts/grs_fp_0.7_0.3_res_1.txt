Universities are meant to be inclusive learning environments where all students have equal opportunities to succeed. However, for disabled students, this may not always be the case. While universities are legally obligated to provide reasonable accommodations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), some may question whether or not universities should go above and beyond these legal requirements to ensure that all students have access to a quality education.

There are several arguments in support of providing accommodations to disabled students at universities. Firstly, it is important for universities to create an inclusive environment where all students feel welcome and supported. By providing accommodations such as accessible classrooms, materials in alternative formats, and assistive technology, universities can show their commitment to diversity and inclusion.

Additionally, providing accommodations can help level the playing field for disabled students who may face barriers in accessing the same opportunities as their non-disabled peers. By removing these barriers, universities can help ensure that all students have an equal chance at academic success.

Moreover, providing accommodations can benefit not only disabled students but also the university community as a whole. By embracing diversity and accommodating different learning styles and needs, universities can create a more supportive and enriching environment for all students.

On the other hand, some may argue that providing accommodations to disabled students is unfair or unequal treatment. They may argue that disabled students should be able to adapt to the same standards and expectations as non-disabled students without needing special treatment.
