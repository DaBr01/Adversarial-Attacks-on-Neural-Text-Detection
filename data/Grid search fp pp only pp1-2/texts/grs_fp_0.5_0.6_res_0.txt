Studies have consistently found a link between exposure to violent media and aggression in children. Furthermore, research has shown that exposure to violent cartoons can also lead to desensitization to violence. This desensitization can make it easier for them to accept or justify violence as a normal part of life.

In addition to increased aggression and desensitization, exposure to violent cartoons can also lead to increased fear and anxiety in children. Watching scary or violent content can be upsetting for young viewers and may cause them to experience nightmares, anxiety, or other negative emotions. Research has shown that repeated exposure to frightening or disturbing content can have detrimental effects on children's mental health.

It is important for parents and caregivers to be aware of the potential impacts of violent cartoons on children and take steps to monitor their media consumption. Limiting screen time, choosing age-appropriate content, and discussing what they see with their child can help mitigate the negative effects of violent media.
