In recent years, there has been an increasing focus on the importance of creating inclusive and accessible environments for individuals with disabilities in all aspects of society. This includes higher education institutions such as universities. According to data from the National Center for Education Statistics, there were over 2 million students with disabilities enrolled in degree-granting postsecondary institutions in the United States during the 2019-2020 academic year. It is crucial that universities provide accommodations to disabled students to ensure equal access to educational opportunities.

One of the main reasons why universities should provide accommodations to disabled students is that it is a legal requirement under various laws including the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. These laws mandate that individuals with disabilities must be provided reasonable accommodations to ensure they have equal access to programs and services offered by public entities such as universities. Failure to comply with these regulations can result in legal consequences for higher education institutions.

Furthermore, providing accommodations to disabled students not only fulfills legal obligations but also promotes diversity and inclusion within university campuses. By addressing barriers that may hinder disabled students' ability to fully participate in academic activities, colleges create a more welcoming environment where all individuals are able to thrive regardless of their physical or mental abilities.

Additionally, research shows that providing accommodations for disabled students positively impacts their academic performance and overall well-being. A study published in The Journal of Postsecondary Education found that students who received appropriate accommodations reported higher levels of satisfaction with their college experience compared to those who did not receive any support.
