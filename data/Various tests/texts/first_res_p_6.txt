As a recent college graduate, I know first-hand the impact that college debt can have on the future lives of students. The burden of student loans can affect various aspects of life, including career choices, financial stability, and overall well-being.

When I was in college, I found myself constantly worrying about the amount of debt I was accumulating. I had to take out loans to cover tuition, books, and living expenses, and the thought of having to pay them back after graduation was always at the back of my mind. This pressure often influenced my academic and career decisions, as I felt pressured to pursue a high-paying job to ensure I could repay my loans in a timely manner.

Many students face similar struggles when it comes to college debt. According to a study conducted by the Institute for College Access & Success, the average student loan debt for graduates in 2021 was over $30,000. This significant financial burden can impact the choices students make after graduation. Some may feel forced to take on multiple jobs or delay important life milestones, such as buying a home or starting a family, in order to repay their loans.

Furthermore, college debt can also affect mental health and well-being. The stress of managing loan payments can lead to anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. In fact, a recent survey by the American Psychological Association found that 64% of adults with student loan debt reported feeling overwhelmed by their debt.
