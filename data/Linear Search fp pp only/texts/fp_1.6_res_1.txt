Universities should provide accommodations to disabled students in order to create a more inclusive and equitable educational environment. While some may argue that universities are not responsible for providing accommodations, it is essential to recognize the needs of all students and ensure they have equal opportunities for success.

One of the primary reasons universities should provide accommodations to disabled students is because it is required by law. The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including education. This means that universities are legally obligated to provide reasonable accommodations to ensure disabled students have equal access to education.

Providing accommodations also aligns with the mission of universities as institutions dedicated to fostering diversity and inclusion. By creating an accessible environment for all students, universities demonstrate their commitment to supporting diversity and ensuring that every student has the opportunity to succeed academically.

Furthermore, providing accommodations can significantly impact a disabled student's academic performance and overall well-being. Accessible resources such as note-taking services, extended time on exams, or specialized equipment can level the playing field for disabled students and enable them to fully participate in their academic experience.

In addition, accommodating disabled students can benefit the entire university community by promoting awareness and understanding of disability rights issues. By creating a more inclusive environment where diverse perspectives are valued, universities foster an atmosphere where all members feel respected and supported.

However, some may argue that providing accommodations places an undue burden on universities in terms of cost or logistical challenges.
