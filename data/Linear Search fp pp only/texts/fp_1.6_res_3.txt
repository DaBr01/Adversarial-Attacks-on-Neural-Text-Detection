Technology and gadgets have become an integral part of our daily lives, including the lives of children. With the increasing availability and accessibility of smartphones, tablets, computers, and other electronic devices, children are spending more time on screens than ever before. While technology can offer many benefits in terms of learning and entertainment, there are also concerns about how these devices may be impacting the studies and overall development of children.

One major concern regarding technology's impact on children's studies is its potential to be a distraction. With so many apps, games, social media platforms available at their fingertips, it can be easy for students to lose focus on their schoolwork. Studies have shown that excessive screen time can lead to decreased attention span and poorer academic performance.

Additionally, the use of technology has been linked to a decline in reading habits among children. Many kids now prefer watching videos or playing games online rather than picking up a book or completing assigned readings for school. This could result in weaker literacy skills over time which could further hinder their academic success.

Moreover,some experts argue that excessive screen time may also contribute to physical health issues such as obesity as sedentary behaviors replace outdoor activities.Inadequate sleep is another major concern associated with using electronic devices before bedtime.The blue light emitted by screens can disrupt sleep patterns causing difficulties falling asleep which ultimately affects learning ability during waking hours.
