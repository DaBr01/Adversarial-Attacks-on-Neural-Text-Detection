Technology and gadgets have become an integral part of our lives, transforming the way we communicate, work, and learn. While technology has provided many benefits it has raised concerns about its impact on children's studies. As digital devices become increasingly prevalent in households and classrooms it is important to understand how they impact on children's academic performance and overall well-being. One of the most significant ways technology affects children's academics is through distraction. With the proliferation of smartphones tablets and laptops children have constant access to a wide range of social media and entertainment platforms. This can lead to increased screen time linked to lower academic achievement and attention problems in children. Studies have shown that excessive screen time can interfere with children's ability to focus, memorize information, and engage in critical thinking. The use of technology and gadgets can also affect children's sleep patterns which are vital for learning and cognitive development. The blue light that is released by electronic devices can disrupt the production of melatonin a hormone that regulates sleep. Research has shown that exposure to screens before bedtime can lead to fragmented sleep and daytime drowsiness resulting in poor academic performance. In addition to distractions and sleep disturbances technology and gadgets can have a negative impact on the social skills of children and a negative impact on their. Social media platforms and online gaming has changed the way children interact with their peers often leading to decreased face-to-face communication and interpersonal relationships. Excessive technology usage can also contribute to feelings of isolation anxiety and depression in children which can hamper their academic success. It is important however to acknowledge that technology and gadgets can have positive effects on children's studies. Educational apps, online resources, and interactive learning platforms can enhance children's understanding of complex concepts and provide personalized learning experiences. Digital devices can also facilitate collaboration and communication among students allowing them to work together more effectively on projects and share ideas. Technology can also help children to develop key skills such as digital literacy critical thinking and problem solving. Children who embrace technology into their study can gain a competitive edge in a rapidly evolving digital world and prepare for future career opportunities. Technology and gadgets have positive and negative impacts on children's studies both positively and negatively. While digital devices can enhance learning experiences and provide access to valuable resources they can also lead to distractions sleep disturbances and social-emotional problems. Parents educators and policy makers must strike a balance between incorporating technology into children's studies and ensuring that it does not impede their academic success and well-being. By promoting responsible and mindful use of technology, we can harness its benefits while minimizing its potential drawbacks on children's studies.
