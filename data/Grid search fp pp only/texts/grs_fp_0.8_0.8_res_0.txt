Effects of violent cartoons on children

Violent cartoons have been a controversial topic for many years, with parents, educators, and psychologists debating the potential impact of these animated shows on young viewers. While some argue that violent cartoons can desensitize children to real-life violence and promote aggressive behavior, others believe that these shows are harmless entertainment. This research essay will explore the effects of violent cartoons on children's behavior, emotions, and attitudes.

One potential effect of watching violent cartoons is an increase in aggressive behavior. Research has shown that exposure to media violence can lead to increased aggression in children. One study found that preschoolers who watched a television show featuring aggressive cartoon characters were more likely to exhibit physical aggression towards their peers compared to children who watched a non-violent cartoon (Lindsay et al., 2015). This suggests that viewing violent content can influence how children think and behave towards others.

In addition to promoting aggression, violent cartoons can also desensitize children to real-life violence. Exposure to graphic images and depictions of violence can lead kids to become desensitized or numb to the suffering of others. This can make them less empathetic and caring individuals in the long run (Bushman & Huesmann, 2018). When children see violence as entertainment without understanding its consequences or impact on others, they may develop a skewed perception of what is acceptable behavior.

Furthermore, watching violent cartoons can have negative effects on children's emotional development. Studies have shown that exposure to violent media content can lead to increased levels of anxiety and fear in young viewers (Gentile et al., 2017). Children may struggle with processing frightening or intense scenes depicted in these shows which could contribute to mental health issues such as anxiety disorders later in life.

Moreover, watching violent cartoons may also influence children's attitudes towards conflict resolution and problem-solving. If kids are exposed regularly to shows where characters use violence as a means of solving problems or conflicts, they may internalize this message and believe that aggression is an acceptable way to resolve disputes (Lester & Bushway, 2019). This could lead them down a dangerous path where they resort quickly resorting To using force instead of seeking peaceful solutions through communication.

In conclusion, while some may argue that watching violent cartoons has little impact on children's behavior or emotions; research suggests otherwise.
